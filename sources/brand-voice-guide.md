## Brand Personality Traits

| Trait | What it means | What it does not mean |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Confident** | We know our product and stand behind it. We make clear recommendations. | We never belittle competitors, dismiss alternatives, or talk down to the reader. |
| **Helpful** | We anticipate what someone needs and offer it proactively. We explain rather than assume. | We never condescend, over-explain the obvious, or use a teacherly tone. |
| **Clear** | We use plain language, short sentences, and concrete examples. | We never dumb things down, strip away necessary nuance, or oversimplify trade-offs. |
| **Warm** | We write like a knowledgeable colleague, not a corporate entity. First names, contractions, friendly phrasing. | We never slip into slang, forced humour, or overly casual language that undermines trust. |
| **Ambitious** | We celebrate what people can achieve. We focus on possibility and progress. | We never hype, exaggerate, or make promises we cannot back up with evidence. |

## Language Rules

- Use active voice wherever possible
- Use British English spelling and conventions
- Avoid corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, paradigm shift)
- Avoid hedging language (might, perhaps, arguably) unless genuinely uncertain
- Use "you" to address the reader directly
- Keep sentences under 25 words on average
- Define technical terms on first use

## Vocabulary Preferences

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| Help, guide, support | Empower, leverage, unlock |
| Build, create, design | Craft (overused), curate |
| Simple, straightforward | Easy (implies no effort needed) |
| Improve, strengthen | Supercharge, turbocharge |
| People, teams, customers | Users, end-users (unless technical context) |
| Works with, connects to | Seamlessly integrates |
| Try, explore, get started | Dive in, take the plunge |
| Challenge, problem | Pain point (internal jargon) |
| Results, outcomes | ROI (unless audience expects it) |
| Show, demonstrate | Showcase |

## Tone Spectrum

Our voice stays consistent, but tone shifts depending on context:

| Context | Register | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Social media** | Casual, punchy. Short sentences. Questions welcome. Contractions always. | "Three audience segments in under a minute. That's what we call a head start." |
| **Email marketing** | Warm, professional. Conversational but structured. Clear CTA. | "Hi Sarah, your Q2 campaign plan is ready for review. We've mapped three segments to four channels — take a look and let us know what you'd adjust." |
| **Website / product pages** | Clear, benefit-focused. Lead with what the reader gains. | "Build campaigns grounded in real audience insight, not guesswork." |
| **Blog posts / articles** | Expert and approachable. Lead with insight, not preamble. | "Most content calendars fail because they plan topics without understanding who they're for. Start with your audience segments." |
| **Help documentation** | Direct, instructional. No filler. Step-by-step where possible. | "Select your audience segment, then choose the channels you want to target. The planner generates a timeline automatically." |
| **Crisis / sensitive comms** | Serious, measured. No humour. Acknowledge the situation plainly. | "We identified an issue affecting exported campaign data on 14 March. Here's what happened, what we've done, and what you need to check." |

## On-Brand vs Off-Brand Examples

**On-brand:** "Campaign planning starts with knowing who you're talking to. Segment your audience first, then let the strategy follow."

**Off-brand:** "Unlock the power of audience-driven campaign ideation to supercharge your marketing pipeline and drive unprecedented engagement."

**On-brand:** "Something went wrong with your export. Here's how to fix it."

**Off-brand:** "Oops! Looks like gremlins got into your export. Don't worry, we've got your back!"

**On-brand:** "This workflow works best with detailed customer data — the more context you provide, the sharper the segments."

**Off-brand:** "Simply input your data and watch the magic happen!"

## Audience-Specific Adjustments

- **Marketing managers:** Assume familiarity with campaign concepts (segmentation, channel mix, KPIs). Skip definitions. Focus on how it saves time and improves outcomes.
- **Small business owners:** Explain marketing terms briefly on first use. Emphasise practical results over methodology. Keep recommendations concrete.
- **Agency teams:** Speak peer-to-peer. They know the craft — focus on efficiency, consistency across clients, and scalability.
- **Content creators:** Respect their craft. Suggest improvements without being prescriptive. Focus on consistency and audience alignment.
- **Executives / stakeholders:** Lead with outcomes and metrics. Keep it concise. Avoid tactical detail unless asked.
